Contest a Citation

You have the right to contest any citation in court.

  1. Plead "Not Guilty" on citation or online
  2. Court date will be assigned
  3. Appear in court on assigned date
  4. Present your case to the judge
  5. Judge will make a ruling

Note: You may request a continuance once if you cannot appear on your court date.

Traffic School

Eligible drivers may attend traffic school to:

  • Dismiss citation
  • Prevent points on license
  • Avoid insurance increase

Eligibility

  • Valid driver's license
  • Not attended traffic school in past 12 months
  • Violation is eligible (ask court clerk)
  • Complete before court date

Cost

Traffic school fee: $75 plus court costs

Warrant Information

Failure to appear in court or pay fines may result in a warrant.

Resolve a Warrant

  • Contact the court immediately
  • Arrange payment or court date
  • Warrants may be recalled upon payment

Check for Warrants

Call the court at (555) 123-6000 to check warrant status.

Small Claims Court

Small claims court handles disputes under $5,000.

File a Small Claim

  • Claim amount must be $5,000 or less
  • Filing fee: $50-$150 based on claim amount
  • Complete small claims petition
  • Serve defendant with notice
  • Attend hearing

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I don't pay my ticket?

Unpaid tickets may result in additional fees, suspension of driving privileges, or a warrant for your arrest.

Can I make payment arrangements?

Yes, contact the court to arrange a payment plan for fines over $200.

Do I need an attorney?

You have the right to an attorney but one is not required for traffic violations or ordinance cases.

What should I bring to court?

Bring your citation, ID, and any evidence or documents related to your case.
