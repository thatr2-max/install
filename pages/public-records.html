Public Records Request

Public Access: Under state law, most city records are public and available for inspection or copying.

Submit a Public Records Request

Request access to public documents, meeting minutes, and city records. All fields marked with * are required.

Please be as specific as possible. Include dates, names, or other identifying information.

Commonly Requested Records

Meeting Minutes

Access official minutes from city council, planning commission, and board meetings.

  • City Council minutes
  • Planning Commission minutes
  • Board & committee minutes
  • Public hearing records

Availability: Immediate for approved minutes

Ordinances & Resolutions

View city ordinances, resolutions, and municipal code.

  • Municipal code
  • Current ordinances
  • Council resolutions
  • Administrative regulations

Availability: Immediate online access

Budget & Financial

Access city budget documents, financial reports, and audit reports.

  • Annual budgets
  • Financial statements
  • Audit reports
  • Expenditure reports

Availability: 1-3 business days

Property Records

Access property tax assessments, zoning information, and permits.

  • Property assessments
  • Zoning information
  • Building permits
  • Code violations

Availability: 1-2 business days

Response Timeline

The city will respond to your public records request within the following timeframes:

  • Initial Response: Within 5 business days
  • Simple Requests: Fulfilled within 10 business days
  • Complex Requests: May require additional time; you will be notified of expected completion date
  • Exempt Records: If records are exempt, you'll receive written explanation within 10 business days

Exempt Records

Certain records may be exempt from disclosure under state law, including:

  • Ongoing criminal investigations
  • Personnel records and employee evaluations
  • Attorney-client privileged communications
  • Trade secrets and proprietary information
  • Student education records
  • Medical records
  • Ongoing litigation materials

If your request includes exempt records, we will provide all non-exempt portions and explain any exemptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do public records cost?

Digital records are free. Paper copies cost $0.10 per page. Research time over one hour is charged at $25/hour.

How long does it take to receive records?

Simple requests are typically fulfilled within 10 business days. Complex requests may take longer.

Can I inspect records in person?

Yes! You can schedule an appointment to inspect records at City Hall during business hours at no charge.

What if my request is denied?

You will receive written explanation of any denial. You may appeal denials through the process outlined in the denial letter.
