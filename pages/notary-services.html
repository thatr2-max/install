Notary Services

Notary Public Services

The City Clerk's office provides notary public services to the public during regular business hours.

What is a Notary Public?

A notary public is an official authorized by the state to witness the signing of important documents and administer oaths. Notarization helps prevent fraud by verifying the identity of signers and their willingness to sign documents.

Services Available

  • Document notarization
  • Acknowledgments
  • Jurats (sworn statements)
  • Copy certifications
  • Oaths and affirmations

Fees

Standard Notarization: $5.00 per signature

Copy Certification: $5.00 per document

Oath/Affirmation: $5.00

How to Get a Document Notarized

What to Bring

  1. The Document: Bring the unsigned document (do not sign before appearing)
  2. Valid Photo ID: Government-issued ID (driver's license, passport, state ID)
  3. Payment: Cash, check, or card accepted

Important Requirements

  • Personal Appearance: You must appear in person before the notary
  • Do Not Sign in Advance: Documents must be signed in the notary's presence
  • Valid Identification: Must present acceptable photo ID
  • Understand the Document: You must understand what you are signing
  • Sign Willingly: You must not be under duress or coercion

Process

  1. Present your unsigned document and valid photo ID
  2. Notary verifies your identity
  3. Sign the document in the notary's presence
  4. Notary completes the notarial certificate
  5. Notary affixes seal and signature
  6. Pay notarization fee

The city does not currently offer remote online notarization (RON). For RON services, contact private notaries or online notary platforms.

Acceptable Identification

You must present one of the following current, valid photo IDs:

Primary Identification

  • Driver's license (any state)
  • State-issued identification card
  • U.S. Passport or Passport Card
  • Military ID (active duty or retired)
  • Permanent Resident Card (Green Card)

Identification Requirements

  • Must be current and valid (not expired)
  • Must contain a photograph
  • Must contain signature
  • Must be issued by government agency

Unacceptable Identification

  • Expired IDs
  • Photocopies of IDs
  • Credit cards or bank cards
  • Employee badges (without government seal)
  • Student IDs

Documents That Cannot Be Notarized

Notaries cannot notarize certain documents:

Prohibited Notarizations

  • Birth Certificates: Cannot be notarized (certified copies available from vital records)
  • Death Certificates: Cannot be notarized
  • Marriage Certificates: Cannot be notarized
  • Court Documents: Must be certified by court clerk
  • Diplomas: Cannot be notarized
  • Photographs: Cannot be notarized
  • Incomplete Documents: All blanks must be filled in

When Notary Must Refuse

A notary must refuse service if:

  • Signer does not have proper identification
  • Signer appears confused or doesn't understand document
  • Signer appears to be under duress
  • Document is incomplete
  • Notary has financial interest in transaction
  • Signer is related to notary (varies by state)

Apostille Services

An apostille is a certificate that authenticates documents for use in foreign countries.

What is an Apostille?

An apostille certifies the authenticity of a document for international use in countries that are part of the Hague Apostille Convention.

How to Obtain an Apostille

  1. Get document notarized (if required)
  2. Contact Secretary of State office for apostille
  3. Submit document with apostille application
  4. Pay apostille fee (varies by state)

Note: Apostilles are issued by the Secretary of State, not the city. The city can notarize your documents before you request an apostille.

Becoming a Notary Public

Interested in becoming a notary public?

Requirements

  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Be a legal resident of the state
  • Pass background check
  • Complete required training
  • Pass state notary exam
  • Purchase notary bond and supplies

Application Process

  1. Complete state notary application
  2. Submit to Secretary of State
  3. Take required training course
  4. Pass notary exam
  5. Purchase $10,000 bond
  6. Take oath of office at county clerk
  7. Purchase notary seal and journal

Notary Commission

Term: 4 years

Renewal: Required before expiration

More Information

Contact the Secretary of State's office for detailed information about becoming a notary public.

Certified Copies

Some documents require certified copies rather than notarized copies.

Vital Records

Certified copies of birth, death, and marriage certificates must be obtained from:

  • County Clerk: Marriage certificates
  • State Vital Records: Birth and death certificates

Notaries cannot certify copies of these documents.

Court Documents

Certified copies of court documents must be obtained from the court that issued the document.

What Notaries CAN Certify

Notaries can certify copies of:

  • Original documents in your possession
  • Contracts
  • Business records
  • Personal records

Note: Requirements vary by state. Some states do not allow notaries to certify copies.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need an appointment?

No appointment is needed at City Hall during regular business hours. However, calling ahead ensures a notary is available.

Can a notary give legal advice?

No. Notaries cannot provide legal advice unless they are also licensed attorneys. Consult an attorney for legal questions.

Can someone else sign for me?

No. You must personally appear and sign the document yourself. Power of attorney does not apply to notarization.

What if my ID is expired?

Expired identification is not acceptable. You must present current, valid ID.

Can documents be notarized via fax or email?

No. Personal appearance is required. Remote online notarization (RON) requires special technology and authorization.

How long does notarization take?

Most notarizations take 5-10 minutes, depending on the complexity of the document.

Can I have documents notarized in a language other than English?

The notary must be able to communicate directly with you. If the document is in a foreign language, the notary may require translation assistance.
