Collection Guidelines

  • Place carts at curb by 7:00 AM on collection day
  • Remove carts from curb by 8:00 PM
  • Cart handles should face house
  • Allow 3 feet clearance around carts
  • Keep carts away from vehicles, mailboxes, poles

Garbage Collection

Weekly curbside collection of household garbage.

What Goes in Your Garbage Cart

  • Household trash
  • Food waste
  • Non-recyclable packaging

  • Pet waste (bagged)

What Does NOT Go in Garbage Cart

  • Yard waste
  • Hazardous waste

Recycling

Weekly single-stream recycling collection.

Accepted Recyclables

Paper & Cardboard

  • Newspaper and magazines
  • Office paper
  • Cardboard boxes (flattened)
  • Paperboard (cereal boxes)
  • Mail and envelopes

Plastic

  • Bottles and jugs (#1, #2)
  • Food containers
  • Detergent bottles
  • Shampoo bottles
  • Empty and rinse

Metal

  • Aluminum cans
  • Steel/tin cans
  • Empty aerosol cans
  • Metal lids
  • Empty and rinse

Glass

  • Bottles and jars
  • All colors accepted
  • Empty and rinse
  • Remove lids/caps

NOT Accepted in Recycling

  • Plastic bags and film
  • Styrofoam
  • Food-soiled paper/cardboard
  • Window glass or mirrors
  • Ceramics or dishes
  • Electronics
  • Batteries
  • Hazardous materials

Recycling Tips

  • Empty and rinse: Remove food residue from containers
  • Keep it dry: Don't bag recyclables
  • Flatten cardboard: Break down boxes
  • No tanglers: Keep plastic bags, hoses, cords out
  • When in doubt, throw it out: Contamination ruins recycling

Yard Waste Collection

Weekly curbside collection of yard waste from April through November.

Accepted Yard Waste

  • Grass clippings
  • Leaves
  • Small branches (under 4 inches diameter)
  • Garden plants and weeds
  • Christmas trees (January only)

Collection Requirements

  • Place in biodegradable paper bags OR reusable container (marked "Yard Waste")
  • Maximum weight: 50 pounds per bag
  • Bundle branches with twine (maximum 4 feet long)
  • No plastic bags
  • No dirt, rocks, or sod

Fall Leaf Collection

Special vacuum collection in October and November.

Rake leaves to curb (not in street). Do not bag leaves for vacuum collection.

Bulk Item Pickup

Monthly collection of large items that don't fit in carts.

Schedule Bulk Pickup

Call (555) 123-4600 or schedule online at least 3 days before your regular collection day.

Schedule Bulk Pickup

Accepted Bulk Items

  • Furniture (sofas, chairs, tables)
  • Mattresses and box springs
  • Appliances (without freon)
  • Water heaters
  • Carpeting (rolled and tied, 4-foot lengths)
  • Exercise equipment

NOT Accepted as Bulk Items

  • Construction debris
  • Hazardous materials
  • Tires
  • Propane tanks
  • Electronics (see electronics recycling)
  • Yard waste

Limits

Residential: 3 cubic yards per pickup (approximately 1 pickup truck load)

Frequency: One bulk pickup per month included; additional pickups $25 each

Report Collection Issues

Report missed pickups or damaged carts.

Missed Collection

If your collection was missed:

  1. Verify it was your scheduled collection day
  2. Check if holiday schedule affected collection
  3. Ensure cart was placed correctly
  4. Report by calling (555) 123-4600 by 5:00 PM on collection day

Damaged or Missing Cart

Report damaged, stolen, or missing carts for replacement.

Fee: Free for first replacement; $50 for subsequent replacements

Waste Reduction Tips

Reduce

  • Buy products with less packaging
  • Use reusable bags, bottles, and containers
  • Avoid single-use items
  • Purchase only what you need

Reuse

  • Donate unwanted items in good condition
  • Repair instead of replace when possible
  • Repurpose items for new uses
  • Shop secondhand

Recycle

  • Know what's recyclable in your area
  • Keep recyclables clean and dry
  • Recycle electronics properly
  • Compost food scraps and yard waste
