Accessibility Statement

Our Commitment to Accessibility

The City Government is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all people, including those with disabilities. We are continually improving the user experience for everyone and applying the relevant accessibility standards to ensure we provide equal access to all our users.

We believe that everyone should be able to access city services, information, and resources, regardless of their abilities. This commitment extends to our website, mobile applications, public facilities, and in-person services.

Conformance Status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. This website strives to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards.

Standards We Follow:

  • WCAG 2.1 Level AA: Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
  • Section 508: Federal accessibility standards
  • ADA Title II: Americans with Disabilities Act compliance

Accessibility Features

Our website includes the following accessibility features:

Navigation & Structure

  • Skip navigation links for screen reader users
  • Logical heading hierarchy (H1-H6)
  • Descriptive link text
  • Consistent navigation across all pages
  • Breadcrumb trails for orientation
  • ARIA landmarks for page regions

Visual Design

  • High contrast text (minimum 4.5:1 ratio)
  • Text resizing up to 200% without loss of functionality
  • Clear focus indicators for keyboard navigation
  • No reliance on color alone to convey information
  • Readable fonts and adequate spacing

Keyboard Accessibility

  • Full keyboard navigation support
  • Visible focus indicators
  • No keyboard traps
  • Logical tab order

Screen Reader Support

  • ARIA labels and roles
  • Alt text for all images
  • Descriptive form labels
  • Table headers and captions
  • Status messages and alerts

Forms & Interactive Elements

  • Clear form labels and instructions
  • Error identification and suggestions
  • Required field indicators
  • Accessible error messages
  • Sufficient time for form completion

Media & Documents

  • Captions for video content
  • Transcripts for audio content
  • Accessible PDF documents
  • Alternative text for images

Assistive Technology Compatibility

This website is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen Readers: JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Screen Magnification: ZoomText, MAGic
  • Speech Recognition: Dragon NaturallySpeaking
  • Keyboard Navigation: All functionality available via keyboard
  • Browser Extensions: Compatible with accessibility browser extensions

Available Accommodations

We provide various accommodations for accessing city services:

Website Accommodations

  • Documents available in alternative formats (large print, braille, audio)
  • Text-only version of pages upon request
  • Assistance with online forms and applications

In-Person Services

  • Sign language interpreters (48-hour notice requested)
  • Assistive listening devices
  • Large print materials
  • Wheelchair accessible facilities
  • Accessible parking
  • Service animal friendly

Public Meetings & Events

  • ASL interpretation upon request
  • Closed captioning for virtual meetings
  • Accessible seating
  • Materials in alternative formats
  • Dietary accommodations

To request accommodations: Contact us at least 48 hours in advance at (555) 123-4567 or accessibility@citygovernment.gov

Third-Party Content

While we strive to ensure accessibility throughout our website, some content may be provided by third parties that are outside our control. We are working with all vendors and partners to ensure their content meets accessibility standards.

Ongoing Efforts

We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of our digital services:

  • Regular accessibility audits and testing
  • Staff training on accessibility best practices
  • User testing with people with disabilities
  • Monitoring and addressing accessibility issues
  • Updating content and features to meet evolving standards
  • Evaluating new technologies for accessibility

Formal Complaints

If you wish to file a formal complaint regarding accessibility:

  1. Contact our ADA Coordinator using the information above
  2. Describe the accessibility barrier in detail
  3. Provide your contact information
  4. You will receive a written response within 10 business days

You also have the right to file a complaint with:

  • U.S. Department of Justice, Civil Rights Division
  • U.S. Department of Health and Human Services, Office for Civil Rights
