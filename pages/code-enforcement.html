Code Enforcement

Our Mission

The Code Enforcement Division works to maintain property standards and ensure compliance with city ordinances, protecting property values and quality of life for all residents.

Common Code Violations

Property Maintenance

  • Overgrown grass/weeds (over 10 inches)
  • Peeling paint or deteriorated structures
  • Broken windows or doors
  • Damaged roofs or siding
  • Junk or debris accumulation

Vehicles

  • Inoperable vehicles on property
  • Unlicensed vehicles parked on street
  • Commercial vehicles in residential areas
  • Abandoned vehicles
  • Parking on lawn or unpaved surfaces

Zoning Violations

  • Unpermitted structures
  • Home businesses without permits
  • Illegal signs
  • Setback violations
  • Improper land use

Nuisances

  • Excessive noise
  • Outdoor storage violations
  • Drainage problems
  • Unsanitary conditions
  • Rodent/pest infestations

Enforcement Process

  1. Complaint Received: Violation reported or observed during inspection
  2. Investigation: Code officer investigates complaint within 3-5 business days
  3. Notice of Violation: If violation confirmed, notice sent to property owner
  4. Compliance Period: Owner given time to correct violation (7-30 days depending on severity)
  5. Re-Inspection: Code officer verifies compliance
  6. Citation: If not corrected, citation issued with fines
  7. Legal Action: Continued non-compliance may result in court action

Property Maintenance Standards

Lawn Care

  • Grass and weeds must be kept below 10 inches
  • Property must be free of noxious weeds
  • Trees and shrubs must not obstruct sidewalks or streets

Exterior Maintenance

  • Paint must be maintained in good condition
  • Roofs must be weather-tight and in good repair
  • Windows and doors must be intact and functional
  • Foundations and walls must be structurally sound

Storage

  • No outdoor storage of junk or inoperable equipment
  • Trash containers must be properly stored when not at curb
  • Construction materials must be properly stored

Vehicle Regulations

Inoperable Vehicles

Vehicles must be operable and currently licensed. Inoperable vehicles include those:

  • Without current license plates
  • With flat tires or missing wheels
  • Obviously incapable of being driven
  • Damaged or wrecked

Parking Regulations

  • Vehicles must be parked on improved surfaces (not grass)
  • Commercial vehicles over 1 ton prohibited in residential zones
  • No parking in required setbacks
  • Maximum of 2 recreational vehicles per property

Penalties & Fines

Code violation fines
Violation Type Fine Amount Compliance Period
Tall Grass/Weeds $100 + abatement costs 7 days
Inoperable Vehicle $150 per vehicle 14 days
Junk/Debris $100-$500 14 days
Property Maintenance $100-$500 30 days
Zoning Violation $200-$1,000 30 days

Fines increase for repeat violations. Each day of continued violation may constitute a separate offense.

Appeals Process

If you disagree with a code violation notice:

  1. File written appeal within 10 days of notice
  2. Include your reasons for appeal and supporting documentation
  3. Appeal will be reviewed by Code Enforcement Supervisor
  4. You will receive written decision within 15 days
  5. If appeal denied, you may request hearing before Board of Appeals

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I report a violation anonymously?

Yes, anonymous reports are accepted. However, providing contact information allows us to update you on the case status.

How long does the property owner have to fix a violation?

Compliance periods range from 7-30 days depending on the type and severity of violation.

What if my neighbor doesn't comply?

If violations are not corrected within the compliance period, we will issue citations and may pursue legal action.

Can code enforcement enter my property?

Code officers may observe violations visible from public areas. Entry onto private property requires permission or a warrant.
