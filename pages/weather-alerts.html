Severe Weather Preparedness

Tornado Safety

Warning Signs: Dark greenish sky, large hail, loud roar

What to Do:

  • Go to lowest level of building
  • Stay away from windows
  • Get under sturdy furniture
  • Cover head and neck
  • Stay in shelter until all clear

Severe Thunderstorm Safety

  • Go indoors immediately
  • Stay away from windows
  • Avoid electrical equipment and plumbing
  • Do not take a shower or bath
  • Unplug sensitive electronics

Lightning Safety

When Thunder Roars, Go Indoors!

  • Seek shelter in substantial building or hard-topped vehicle
  • Stay inside for 30 minutes after last thunder
  • If caught outside: crouch low, minimize contact with ground
  • Never shelter under trees

Winter Weather Preparedness

Before Winter Weather

  • Service snow removal equipment
  • Stock up on salt/sand for walkways
  • Have generators serviced
  • Prepare emergency kit (flashlights, batteries, blankets)
  • Check heating system
  • Insulate pipes

During Winter Storms

  • Stay indoors if possible
  • Dress in layers if going outside
  • Avoid overexertion when shoveling
  • Keep dry and change wet clothing promptly
  • Drive only if necessary

Winter Weather Terms

Understanding winter weather alerts

Extreme Heat Preparedness

Heat Safety Tips

  • Stay in air-conditioned buildings
  • Drink plenty of water (even if not thirsty)
  • Avoid strenuous activity during hottest hours
  • Wear lightweight, light-colored clothing
  • Never leave children or pets in vehicles
  • Check on elderly neighbors

Heat-Related Illness

Heat Exhaustion

Symptoms: Heavy sweating, weakness, nausea, headache

Treatment: Move to cool place, drink water, apply cool cloths

Heat Stroke

Symptoms: High body temp, confusion, loss of consciousness

Treatment: Call 911 immediately, move to cool place, cool body rapidly

Cooling Centers

When extreme heat warnings are issued, the city opens cooling centers at:

  • Community Center - 500 Park Avenue
  • Public Library - 200 Library Lane
  • Senior Center - 300 Elder Street

Hours: 10:00 AM - 8:00 PM during heat emergencies

Flooding Preparedness

Flash Flood Safety

  • Never drive through flooded roadways
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Stay out of flood-prone areas
  • Monitor weather updates
  • Be prepared to evacuate

Flood Terms

Flood Watch: Flooding is possible

Flood Warning: Flooding is occurring or imminent

Flash Flood Warning: Rapid flooding in small creeks, streets, or low areas

Emergency Supplies Kit

Be prepared for severe weather with an emergency supplies kit containing:

Basic Supplies

  • Water (1 gallon per person per day for 3 days)
  • Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • Battery or hand-crank radio (NOAA Weather Radio)
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • First aid kit
  • Medications (7-day supply)
  • Cell phone with chargers and backup battery

Additional Items

  • Important documents in waterproof container
  • Cash and credit cards
  • Blankets or sleeping bags
  • Change of clothing
  • Fire extinguisher
  • Matches in waterproof container
  • Pet supplies if applicable
