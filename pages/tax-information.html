Property Taxes

Property taxes fund essential city services including schools, public safety, roads, and parks.

Current Tax Rate

FY 2025 Tax Rate: $1.25 per $100 of assessed value

How Property Taxes Are Calculated

  1. Property is assessed by the County Assessor
  2. Assessment is multiplied by the tax rate
  3. Exemptions and deductions are applied
  4. Final tax amount is determined

Example Calculation

Assessed Value: $200,000

Tax Rate: $1.25 / $100

Annual Tax: $2,500

Tax Exemptions & Relief Programs

You may qualify for property tax exemptions or relief programs.

Homestead Exemption

Reduces assessed value by $25,000 for primary residences.

Eligibility: Must be primary residence as of January 1st

Apply

Senior Citizen Exemption

Additional $10,000 exemption for residents age 65+.

Eligibility: Age 65+ and income limits apply

Apply

Disabled Veteran Exemption

Exemptions based on disability rating.

Eligibility: Honorably discharged with service-connected disability

Apply

Disability Exemption

Exemption for permanently disabled residents.

Eligibility: Must meet disability criteria

Apply

Application Deadline

Exemption applications must be filed by April 1st to apply for the current tax year.

Tax Appeal Process

If you believe your property assessment is incorrect, you may file an appeal.

How to Appeal

  1. Review your property assessment notice
  2. Gather supporting documentation (recent appraisal, comparable sales)
  3. Complete appeal form
  4. Submit appeal by deadline
  5. Attend appeal hearing if requested

Appeal Deadline: 30 days from assessment notice date

Commercial Property Tax

Commercial properties are assessed differently than residential properties. Contact the Assessor's office for commercial property valuations.

Business Personal Property Tax

Businesses must file annual personal property tax returns for equipment, furniture, and fixtures.

Delinquent Taxes

Unpaid property taxes become delinquent after the due date.

Penalties & Interest

  • Late Payment Penalty: 1.5% per month
  • Interest Rate: 12% per year
  • Collection Costs: Additional fees may apply

Tax Liens

Delinquent taxes result in a tax lien on the property. Continued non-payment may lead to tax foreclosure proceedings.

Frequently Asked Questions

When will I receive my tax bill?

Tax bills are mailed in early November and are due by January 31st.

What if I don't receive my tax bill?

You are still responsible for payment even if you don't receive a bill. Contact our office or view your bill online.

Can I pay my taxes in installments?

Payment plans may be available for those who cannot pay in full. Contact our office to discuss options.

How do I change my mailing address?

Contact the County Assessor's office to update your mailing address for tax bills.
