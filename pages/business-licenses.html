Business Licenses

Annual Renewal Deadline: December 31, 2025
All business licenses expire December 31st and must be renewed annually

Business License Overview

All businesses operating within city limits are required to obtain a business license.

Who Needs a Business License?

  • Retail stores and shops
  • Restaurants and food service
  • Professional services (law, accounting, consulting)
  • Contractors and trades
  • Home-based businesses
  • Mobile businesses
  • Temporary/seasonal businesses

License Fees

License fees are based on number of employees and business type.

Annual business license fees
Number of Employees Annual Fee
Home-based (no employees) $50
1-5 employees $100
6-25 employees $250
26-50 employees $500
51+ employees $750

Apply for New Business License

Complete the application process to obtain your business license.

Application Requirements

  1. Complete Application: Online or paper form
  2. Business Information: Name, address, ownership structure
  3. Owner Identification: Driver's license or state ID
  4. Zoning Compliance: Verify business is allowed in location
  5. State Licenses: Copy of state/federal licenses if applicable
  6. Insurance: Proof of business insurance
  7. Payment: Application fee and annual license fee
Apply Online Download Paper Application

Application Processing

Processing Time: 5-10 business days

Inspection: Some business types require inspection before license issuance

Special License Types

Food Service License

Required for restaurants, cafes, food trucks, and catering businesses.

Additional Requirements:

  • Health Department inspection
  • Food handler certification
  • Fire safety inspection

Fee: $200 + business license fee

Alcohol License

Required to sell or serve alcoholic beverages.

Types:

  • On-premises consumption
  • Off-premises sales
  • Special event permit

Fee: $500-$1,500 + business license fee

Alcohol License Info

Contractor License

Required for construction, electrical, plumbing, HVAC contractors.

Requirements:

  • State contractor license
  • Liability insurance ($500K minimum)
  • Workers' compensation insurance
  • Bond ($10,000)

Fee: $150 + business license fee

Home Occupation License

For businesses operated from residential property.

Restrictions:

  • No exterior signage
  • Limited customer traffic
  • No inventory storage visible from street
  • No employees (except residents)

Fee: $50

Renew Business License

Business licenses expire December 31st and must be renewed annually.

Renewal Process

Renewal notices are mailed in November. You can renew:

  • Online: Fast and convenient (available 24/7)
  • By Mail: Mail renewal form and payment
  • In Person: Visit City Hall, Room 200
Renew License Online

Late Renewal

Licenses not renewed by December 31st are subject to penalties:

  • January 1-31: 10% late fee
  • February 1+: 25% late fee + possible citation

Changes to Business

Notify us of any changes during renewal:

  • Business name or ownership
  • Business address or location
  • Number of employees
  • Type of business activity

Additional Permits & Requirements

  • Mechanical Permit: For HVAC systems
  • Sign Permit: For exterior signage
Building Permits

  • Health Permit: Food service establishments

  • Occupancy Permit: Certificate of occupancy for new buildings

Business License Enforcement

Operating without a valid business license is a violation of city code.

Penalties

  • Fine up to $500 per violation
  • Cease operations order
  • Daily penalties for continued operation

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a business license for an online business?

Yes, if you live in the city and operate a business from your home, you need a home occupation license.

Can I operate a business from my home?

Yes, with a home occupation license, subject to restrictions on signage, employees, and customer traffic.

How long does it take to get a license?

Most applications are processed within 5-10 business days. Some business types requiring inspections may take longer.

Is my business license fee tax deductible?

Consult with your tax advisor. Most business-related fees are deductible as business expenses.

What if I'm just starting and haven't made any money yet?

You must obtain a license before beginning operations, even if you haven't generated revenue yet.
