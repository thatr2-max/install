Safety Resources

Crime Prevention Tips

  • Lock doors and windows
  • Install outdoor lighting
  • Join neighborhood watch
  • Report suspicious activity
  • Secure valuables
  • Get to know your neighbors

Fire Safety Tips

  • Install smoke alarms on every level
  • Test alarms monthly
  • Have a fire escape plan
  • Keep fire extinguisher accessible
  • Never leave cooking unattended
  • Clear dryer lint regularly

Emergency Preparedness

  • Create emergency kit (water, food, supplies)
  • Have family communication plan
  • Know evacuation routes
  • Keep important documents safe
  • Maintain emergency contacts list
  • Sign up for emergency alerts

Community Programs

Neighborhood Watch

Join your neighbors in creating a safer community. Contact the Police Department to start a watch group in your neighborhood.

Citizens Police Academy

Learn about police operations, procedures, and community policing. Free 10-week course offered twice yearly.

Free Smoke Alarms

Qualifying residents can receive free smoke alarms and installation. Call the Fire Department for details.

Monthly classes open to the public. Certification provided upon completion. $30 fee.
