Veterans Services

Thank you for your service! The City is proud to support our veterans and their families.

Veterans Services Office

Veterans Service Officer: John Miller, USMC (Ret.)

Phone: (555) 123-VETS (8387)

Email: veterans@citygovernment.gov

Office Hours: Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM

Walk-ins Welcome

Services Provided

VA Benefits Assistance

  • Help filing VA claims
  • Disability compensation
  • Pension claims
  • Appeals assistance
  • Benefits counseling

Employment

  • Job placement assistance
  • Resume writing
  • Interview preparation
  • Veterans preference info
  • City job opportunities

Healthcare

  • VA healthcare enrollment
  • Referrals to VA facilities
  • Mental health resources
  • PTSD support
  • Substance abuse help

Housing

  • VA home loans
  • Housing assistance
  • Homeless prevention
  • Emergency shelter
  • Transitional housing

City Benefits for Veterans

  • Property Tax Exemption: Disabled veterans may qualify for property tax reduction
  • Employment Preference: 5-10 point preference for city jobs
  • License Plate Discount: Free disabled veteran plates
  • Recreation Discount: 25% off recreation programs
  • Cemetery Plot: Free burial plot for honorably discharged veterans

Documents Needed

When visiting our office, bring:

  • DD-214 (Certificate of Release from Active Duty)
  • Photo ID
  • Social Security card
  • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
  • Medical records (if filing disability claim)

Veterans Memorial

Honor our fallen heroes at the City Veterans Memorial, located in Memorial Park at 555 Memorial Lane.

  • Open daily, dawn to dusk
  • Names of local veterans who made the ultimate sacrifice
  • Annual wreath-laying ceremony
