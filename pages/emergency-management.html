Emergency Shelters

During emergencies, the city may open public shelters. Designated shelter locations:

  • Central High School - 500 School Drive (Primary shelter, 500 capacity)
  • Community Center - 100 Community Way (200 capacity)
  • Westside Elementary - 200 West Street (300 capacity)

Shelters are opened based on emergency type and severity. Bring:

  • Medications and medical supplies
  • Bedding and pillows
  • Change of clothes
  • Important documents
  • Pet supplies (if pet-friendly shelter)

Accessibility: All shelters are ADA accessible and equipped to serve people with disabilities.

Evacuation Routes

Know your evacuation routes and zones:

  • Zone A (Flood-prone areas): Evacuate to higher ground via Highway 50 North
  • Zone B (Low-lying areas): Use Main Street to Route 12 East
  • Zone C (River areas): Take Oak Boulevard to Highway 50 South
View Evacuation Map

During evacuations:

  • Follow official routes
  • Bring emergency kit
  • Secure your home
  • Take pets with you
  • Check on neighbors
  • Don't return until authorities say it's safe

Specific Hazards

Severe Weather

  • Tornado Warning: Seek shelter immediately in basement or interior room
  • Severe Thunderstorm: Move indoors, avoid windows
  • Flood Warning: Move to higher ground, never drive through water
  • Winter Storm: Stay indoors, conserve heat, avoid travel

Extreme Heat

  • Stay hydrated
  • Avoid outdoor activity during hottest hours
  • Check on elderly neighbors
  • Cooling centers open during heat advisories

Power Outages

  • Report outages: (555) 123-POWER
  • Use flashlights (not candles)
  • Keep refrigerator/freezer closed
  • Turn off appliances
  • Never use generators indoors

After an Emergency

Recovery Steps

  1. Wait for all-clear from officials
  2. Check for injuries
  3. Assess damage to your property
  4. Document damage (photos)
  5. Contact insurance company
  6. Watch for hazards (gas leaks, downed power lines)
  7. Begin cleanup safely
  8. Apply for disaster assistance if available

Disaster Assistance

If disaster is declared:

  • Register for FEMA assistance: 1-800-621-FEMA
  • Contact insurance company immediately
  • Document all losses
  • Keep receipts for emergency expenses
  • Visit local disaster recovery center
