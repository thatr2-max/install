Water Treatment Process

  1. Source: River and groundwater wells
  2. Screening: Remove large debris
  3. Coagulation: Particles bind together
  4. Sedimentation: Particles settle
  5. Filtration: Remove remaining particles
  6. Disinfection: Kill bacteria and viruses
  7. Distribution: Delivered to homes and businesses

Water Conservation

Every drop counts. Help us conserve our water resources.

Indoor Water Saving Tips

  • Fix leaky faucets and toilets promptly
  • Install low-flow showerheads and faucet aerators
  • Run dishwasher and washing machine only when full
  • Take shorter showers (5 minutes or less)
  • Turn off water while brushing teeth
  • Install high-efficiency toilets

Outdoor Water Saving Tips

  • Water lawn early morning or evening
  • Use rain barrels to collect water for plants
  • Install drip irrigation systems
  • Use mulch to retain soil moisture
  • Choose native, drought-tolerant plants
  • Don't overwater your lawn

Sewer Services

The city maintains over 300 miles of sanitary sewer lines.

What Goes Down the Drain

Flush ONLY:

  • Human waste
  • Toilet paper

DO NOT Flush:

  • Wipes (even "flushable" wipes)
  • Paper towels or tissues
  • Feminine hygiene products
  • Cotton swabs or dental floss
  • Medications
  • Grease or cooking oil

Grease and FOG (Fats, Oils, Grease)

Never pour grease down drains. Grease solidifies in pipes and causes backups.

Proper Disposal: Pour cooled grease into container and dispose in trash.

Sewer Lateral Responsibility

Property owners are responsible for maintaining the sewer lateral from the house to the main sewer line.

What is a Sewer Lateral?

The pipe connecting your home's plumbing to the city's main sewer line.

Maintenance Responsibility

  • Property Owner: Lateral from house to property line
  • City: Main sewer line in street

Sewer Lateral Insurance

Consider sewer lateral insurance to cover repair costs. Repairs can cost $3,000-$10,000.

Signs of Sewer Lateral Problems

  • Slow drains throughout house
  • Gurgling sounds from drains
  • Sewage backup in lower levels
  • Wet spots in yard
  • Foul odors

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my water discolored?

Usually caused by sediment in pipes, often after water main work. Run cold water until clear. If problem persists, contact us.

Who do I call for a sewer backup?

Call (555) 123-4590 immediately. If backup is in your lateral (private portion), you'll need to call a plumber.

Can I pay my bill in installments?

Payment plans may be available for customers experiencing financial hardship. Contact customer service.

How do I check for leaks?

Turn off all water in your home. If your water meter is still running, you have a leak.
